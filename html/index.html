Shape and size of the Snow Owl

Snowy Owls are very large owls with smoothly rounded heads and no ear tufts.
The body is bulky, with dense feathering on the legs that makes the bird look
white at the base when sitting on the ground.


Color Pattern

Snowy Owls are white birds with varying amounts of black or brown markings on the body and wings.
On females this can be quite dense, giving the bird a salt-and-pepper look.
Males tend to be paler and become whiter as they age.
The eyes are yellow.

Behavior

Look for Snowy Owls sitting on or near the ground in wide-open areas.
They often perch on rises such as the crests of dunes, or on fenceposts, telephone poles, and hay bales.
When they fly they usually stay close to the ground.
